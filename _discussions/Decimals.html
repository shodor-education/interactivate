Mentor: Let's review what the numbers mean. In a fraction, the denominator represents how many equal sized pieces a group or a whole is broken into; which in this problem is 4. The numerator then represents how many of those equal sized pieces there are, in this case 1. If you think about it this way, you can see the similarity between the idea of a fraction and concept of division.

Now let's see how the fraction notation can be represented as division. The number on the outside of the division symbol represents how many equal sized pieces we are trying to divide the whole into, i.e the denominator. The number on the inside of the division symbol represents how many of these equal sized pieces there are, so that is the numerator.
