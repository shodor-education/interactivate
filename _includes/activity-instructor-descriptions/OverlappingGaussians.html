The Overlapping Gaussian applet is designed to allow the user to shift the parameters on two Gaussian curves and then determine whether a difference in the data sets represented by the curves is likely to exist or not. This activity measures the area under the Gaussian curves, which is a tool used to calculate probabilities in normally distributed data.

This activity would work well for individuals or in groups of two to three for about twenty-five to thirty-five minutes if you use the exploration questions.
