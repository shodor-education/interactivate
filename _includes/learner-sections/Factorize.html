This activity allows the user to find all of the factorizations of a given number and then draw the product as the area of a rectangle on a grid. Users can choose to enter in their own numbers and can also receive hints if they are having trouble.

Factors are numbers that when multiplied together form a unique product. For example, 2 factors of 15 are 3 and 5 because 3 * 5 = 15. Every number has at least 2 factors, 1 and the number since 1 multiplied by any number is itself (though when finding the factors of 1, those two are the same number).

A number that only has two factors, one and itself, is called a prime number. However, some numbers have many factors. For example, the factors of 24 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, and 24.
