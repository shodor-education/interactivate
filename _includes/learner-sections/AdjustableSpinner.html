This applet allows the user to explore experimental and theoretical probabilities by changing the area of the colored regions of the circle. When a section is added it reduces the other sections by an equal amount in order to add the new sector. When this occurs, the probability that that particular color will be landed on will change.

Theoretical probability is the probability based on the size of the sector compared to the whole circle. So if there are two equal size sectors you would expect it to land on one of the sectors one out of two spins. The experimental probability is the actual outcome after running an experiment a given number of trials.
