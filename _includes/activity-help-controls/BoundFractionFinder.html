This activity requires you to perform four steps. First, you have to enter values for the two endpoint fractions, decimals, or percentages, with the smaller fraction, decimal, or percentage being entered first. The fractions and decimals must be greater than 0 and less than 1. Next, you create diagrams of the two entered endpoint fractions, decimals, or percentages by dividing a square or circle into equal parts and coloring in the appropriate areas. Then, you have to create a diagram of a third fraction, decimal, or percentage that is between the two endpoints. Finally, you have to give the name, in lowest terms, of your middle fraction, decimal, or percentage.

  • You can choose the game type by clicking one of the radio buttons next to Fraction, Decimal, or Percentage.
  • First enter in the value of the Left (smaller) fraction, decimal, or percentage, using integers for the numerator and the denominator, two digits of precision for the decimal (or three digits if you wish to have a repeating decimal), or a percentage. Press the Set button, then enter the Right fraction, and press the Set button again.
  • You can choose from two different types of fraction, decimal, or percentage diagrams in the Choose Shape pull-down menu: square or circle. If you change shapes in the middle of a problem, all your work to that point will be lost.
  • Below is an example of the square fraction diagram. You can change the number of columns by using the -Col button to remove columns and the +Col button to add columns. You can change the number of rows by using the +Row and -Row buttons. To color a section in the diagram, click the section. You can un-color a section by clicking it again.
  • Below is an example of the circular fraction diagram. You can change the number of sections by using the +Sec button to add sections and the -Sec button to remove sections. To color a section in the diagram, click the section. You can un-color a section by clicking it again.
  • For each of the two endpoint fractions, decimals, or percentages, you should make the colored part of the diagram equal to the corresponding fraction, decimal, or percentage. Then click the Check button for each diagram to check your answer. If the diagram is correct, the word "Correct" will be printed on top of the diagram; if it isn't correct, the word "Incorrect" will be printed instead. If your answer is incorrect, you can try again.
  • After you correctly diagram the two endpoints, you will be asked to diagram a new fraction, decimal, or percentage between the endpoints. You can use any answer you want as long as it is between the two endpoints. When you think your answer is correctly colored and between the two endpoints, click the Check button to see if you are correct.
  • After you correctly diagram the middle fraction, decimal, or percentage you will be asked to name it. In the indicated box, type the middle fraction in lowest terms, the middle decimal with two digits of precision, or the middle percentage, then click the Check button. If you wish to indicate a repeating decimal, use three digits of precision instead of two.
  • At any time during the activity, you can click the Start Over button or the Next Question button. The Start Over button will restart the current question from the very beginning. The Next Question button will set up for a new question with new boundary values.
  • This activity will automatically record how successful you are at answering the questions. To view the score, press the Show Score button at the bottom of the activity and a pop-up window will appear with the scoreboard. To close this pop-up window press the Close button or click back on the main window.
  • To pause the scoring, press the Active button at the bottom of the screen and it will change to a Paused button. To resume scoring, press the Paused button.
  • To reset the scoreboard, open the scoreboard using the Show Score button and then press the Reset button.
